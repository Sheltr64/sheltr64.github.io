I tried (and failed) to get into Pierce the Veil

My friend is a huge emo-pop fan. He's been looking for someone to talk music with for quite some time

I decided to pick up Pierce the Veils "Selfish Machines" (left) and "Collide With The Sky" (right)

I won't deny that the quality of music was there, but nothing felt really that "special" I know this is a gross misrepresentation, but I swear I was listening to My Chemical Romance, a band I found to be a lot more interesting as a whole. I digress, the whole focus on the entry should be on the music. Which is great! I loved the over top preformances of Vic Fuentes and his extremely high vocal registers. Although after my 2 hour long marithon, it got to the point where the insrumentals started to all blend together.

for me, the best songs are:

  • Caraphernelia
  • King For A Day
  • Hell Above
  • Besitos

I got diagnosed with a huge case of the "Cool but its not for me's" which is a shame, because the same hyperdramatic lyrics that make my eyes roll now, would have been happily welcomed by 14 year old me. It definitely is a solid outfit that I do respect.

overall. 7/10

I hate HP

I spent last night looking up how to install Linux Mint on my friends PC, should be easy right? Set up Ventoy, drag and drop the ISO to the USB stick, boot into it and we're in the clear, right? WRONG apparently his PC had a Dell Optane, Which for a TLDR is bascially this:

  • Acts as controller for acting as a "Fake SSD" to make a normal spinning HDD speed up a little bit more (imagine a fast disk dedicated to being a page file)
  • It was used by OEMs in the late 2010's because of the high prices of normal SSDs
  • It quickly fell out of fasion when SSDs became cheaper
  • All the drivers are completely proprietary

That last part is the most important and because of it, Mint was having a hard time understanding what it was working with, abruptly ending my installation process, telling me to disable RAID which the PC doesn't have, so I asked my good friend ChatGPT, to save the day.

Here's a little life lession from me, don't use a LLM to help you solve a problem that doesn't have a clear issue

I went on this wild goose chase for several hours trying to diagnose my issue. Thinking the problem was RAID, I scoured the deepest depths of shudder REDDIT; on there I went on this endless cycle of enabling and disabling security features on the PC. Safeboot, fastboot, virtualization support (I think I forgot to re-enable it on his PC, sorry), boot keys, and much much more. NOTHING.

In the end, it was this page that tipped my off that something could have been a hardware issue

The thing is, my USB stick was also a 16GB drive, so I assumed that the PC was simply picking up on that, not knowing that I just diagnosed the root cause. Yes I know it says NVME, but to play devils advocate here, I was simply being lazy.


live footage of me trying to get my brain to do something

In the end, I did end up opening up the PC, but I was amazed to what I saw: a CD drive. This came as a massive suprise as I had been wanting a way to rip my old CDs for a long time now. Digging a little bit deeper into the PC (and removing all the proprietary screws) I got to the NVME slot, removed the horrible disease that plagued the desktop, and put everything back together.

Here is a picture of the drive

Was this worth is?

Hard YES. Not only did Mint essentially breathe new life into his system, but I also got to rip a few of my discs (shoutout Sufjan Stevens)

Hopefully I can play more games with him without having his PC die in every game we try something new
